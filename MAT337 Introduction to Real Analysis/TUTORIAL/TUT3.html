TUT3

  1. A closed subset $C\subseteq \mathbb{R}$ is complete.

proof. Let $\{x_n\}$ be Cauchy, then it's convergent, since closed, its convergent limit is in $C$.

  1. $S = \{\sum^n_{k=1} k^{-2}: n\in\mathbb{N}^+\}$

Not open: $1+\epsilon$ is not in the set.

  1. An arbitrary intersection $\cap_{n\geq 1}C_n$ of compact sets $C_n$ is compact.

proof. Compact $\Rightarrow C_n$ closed $\Rightarrow$ $\cap C_n$ closed.
Also, $\cap C_n$ is bounded by any $C_n$

  1. $\mathbb{Q}\cap[0,1]$ is not compact

Consider the sequence $x_1 = 3, x_2 = 3.1, x_3 = 3.14, ...$
$x_n\rightarrow \pi\in\mathbb{Q}^c$. This sequence is monotone increasing, All its subsequence then must convergent to $\pi$
