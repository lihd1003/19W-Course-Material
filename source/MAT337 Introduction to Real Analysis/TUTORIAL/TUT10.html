TUT10

Claim $\sum x^k e^{-kx}$ converges uniformly for $x\in\mathbb R^+$.

lemma $\|x^k e^{-kx}\|_{\infty} \leq e^{-k}$
proof. Consider $f(x):=x^k e^{-kx}$, by critical points, $f'(x) = kx^{k-1}e^{-kx} -k x^ke^{-kx} = ke^{-kx}(x^{k-1} - x^k)$ has critical point at $1$ for any $k \geq 1$, (0 cannot hold for $0^0 - 0^1 = 1$). So that $\sup |x^k e^{-kx}| \leq e^{-k}$. By M-test, converges uniformly.

Claim $\sum (x^2 + n^2)^{-1}$
proof. Since $\sup(x^2 + n^2)^{-1} \leq n^{-2}$ and $\sum n^{-2} = \pi^2 / 6 < \infty$, by M-test, converges uniformly.

Example $\sum k^3 x^k$
proof. $\lim |\frac{(k+1)^3}{k^3}| = 1\Rightarrow \lim |k^3|^{1/k} = 1\Rightarrow R = 1$

Example $\sum \frac{(-1)^k}{(2k)!}x^{2k}$
proof. $\lim |(\frac{(2(k+1))!)^{-1}}{(2k!)^{-1}}|= \lim((2k+1)(2k+2))^{-1} = 0\Rightarrow R = \infty$

Example $\sum \frac{k!}{k^k}x^k$
\begin{align*} \lim\sup |\frac{k!}{k^k}|^{1/k} &\sim \lim\sup |\frac{\sqrt{2\pi k}(\frac{k}{e})^k}{k^k}|^{1/k} \\ &= \lim\sup (2\pi k)^{1/2k}e^{-1} \\ &= e^{-1} \lim\sup (2\pi k)^{\frac{1}{2k}} \\ \lim\sup\frac 1{2k}\log(2\pi k) = 0 &\Rightarrow \lim\sup(2\pi k)^{1/2k} = 1 \\ R^{-1} = e^{-1}&\Rightarrow R = e \end{align*}

Example $f(x) = \sum (\frac{1}{(k-1)!} + k)x^{k-1}$
proof. Consider $F(x) :=\int_0^x f(t)dt = \sum (\frac{1}{k!} + 1)x^k$.
\begin{align*} R_1 &= \lim |\frac{((k+1)!)^{-1}}{(k!)^{-1}}| \\ &= \lim (k+1)^{-1} \\ &= 0 \\ R_2 &= \lim 1 = 1 \end{align*}
